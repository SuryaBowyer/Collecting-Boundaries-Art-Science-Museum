<p>This repository includes the datasets for my PhD project, "Illustrations, Documents, Tools: Collecting the Boundaries of Art in the Science Museum, London". The Arts and Humanities Research Council funded this research as part of its Collaborative Doctoral Partnerships scheme, grant number AH/V004549/1. The project was hosted jointly by Science Museum Group and Birkbeck, University of London.</p>
</p>The datasets are described briefly below. Each dataset also includes an "About" sheet which provides further information about how it was created and categorised.</p> 

<strong>Dataset: Science Museum Art Collection</strong> 
<p>This dataset represents the Science Museum's Art Collection. The data is a copy of all object records created and held by the Science Museum in relation to its Art Collection.</p>
</p>The data was exported from the Museum’s collection management system, Mimsy XG. I then cleaned, categorised, and analysed the data in various ways: this is fully documented in the About sheet in the dataset.</p>

<strong>Dataset: National Gallery of Portraits of Inventors, Discoverers, and Introducers of Useful Arts</strong> 
<p>This dataset has been created from the manuscript accessions register of the National Gallery of Portraits of Inventors, Discoverers, and Introducers of Useful Arts. The accessions register is held by the Science Museum Group, File Z29. For many years this collection of portraits was exhibited in the Patent Museum, South Kensington (1858–1883). In 1883 the Patent Museum's collections were transferred to the South Kensington Museum, the antecedent to the Science Museum, London.</p>   
</p>The About sheet in the dataset documents how I adapted information from the register to produce the dataset. I have categorised and analysed the data in various ways: this is also fully documented in the About sheet.</p>

<strong>Dataset: Pictures Loaned to the Science Museum for “A Word to the Mermaids” (1973)</strong> 
<p>This dataset has been created from the exhibition file for “A Word to the Mermaids”, held by the Science Museum, File 5265.</p> 
</p>The About sheet in the dataset documents how I adapted information from the exhibition file to produce the dataset.</p> 

<strong>Dataset: Science Museum Collection Numbers, 1989–1998</strong> 
<p>Researchers working with archival sources and files held by the Science Museum Group often encounter collection numbers. These numbers are no longer widely used by SMG, however in older documentation they are often employed without an accompanying reference to the collection name. This means that identifying to which collection a given number refers can be a difficult task.</p> 
</p>This dataset aims to make that task a little easier. The dataset is based on two sources: (1) a list of Science Museum collections dated 27 June 1989, a scan of which was provided to the author by staff member John Liffen; and (2) the National Museum of Science & Industry Acquisition and Disposal Policy for the period April 1995 — March 1998 (hereafter NMSI A&DP 1995–1998).</p>  
<p>The About sheet in the dataset documents how I adapted and compiled information from the sources to produce the dataset.</p>
